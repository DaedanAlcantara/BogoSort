## Introduction
Some questions are not in need of asking due to practicality, but serves as a cornerstone to pedagogy and development. Such is Bogosort, a highly inefficient joke algorithm that serves no practical purpose but to show what not to do in the spirit of effective algorithm development. In this analysis, the researchers observed and measured the significant time complexity and structure of bogosort against other existing esoteric algorithms to define which properties of the algorithm makes it inefficient and slow in performance. Specific parameters in the analysis and experimental cases include taking a sortable list of integers and applying algorithmic procedures until the elements are sorted – defined as having the nth element smaller than its n+1th element thus, consequent elements in ascending order. Test cases indicate a significant degree of inefficiency in bogosort compared to algorithms such as Thanos Sort and Stalin Sort wherein faster execution is exchanged with lossy performance. 

Meanwhile, Sleep Sort is able to achieve high data integrity but fails to be less effective than bogosort. It is with Bogobogosort where the recursive nature of the strange sorting algorithm exponentially advances bogosort in terms of inefficiency. Though not considered as the most inefficient, Bogosort is a pioneer in the realm of joke algorithms. Serving one purpose – to teach about efficacy in the spirit of the opposite. To show what not to do when designing an algorithm.
